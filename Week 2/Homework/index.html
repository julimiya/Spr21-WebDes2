Andagi

Andagi (also called Sata Andagi in Okinawa) is a fried Okinawan doughnut that's about the size of a golf ball. (although I've seen some the size of a Lacrosse ball) It has a crispy crust, and inside is cake-like. They are not light and fluffy like a yeast doughnut. They are hearty and moist, and more akin to a cake doughnut (but with this crazy, crunchy crust). Andagi must be eaten hot. A well-made andagi is round with a small tail. This tail is created when you drop/flick the shaped dough into the bubbling oil with the flick of your wrist. This tail part is extra crunchy and so good to eat.
