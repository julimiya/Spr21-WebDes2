History

There’s a popular legend surrounding andagi that hints at its deep cultural history and ties to the Ryukyu Islands of Okinawa. A powerful Ryukyuan king was said to have fallen in love with a maiden girl, but she was already in love with a lowly samurai boy. The King, outraged, banished the samurai boy, but before he was removed from the Islands, his mother prepared him a batch of sweet, fried dough balls with a gold coin secreted inside each one. As the samurai’s ship left the Islands forever, he bit into the treats and discovered the coins. The money allowed him to live a comfortable life away from his home. This is why andagi are often referred to as “koban age,” which translates to “fried gold coin.”
